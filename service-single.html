Amblyopia: what is it?

Amblyopia is when there is a difference in vision between the two eyes.
One eye sees well, the other sees badly: it is called "lazy eye".The images transmitted by the eye are of insufficient quality to be processed by the brain. It will therefore ignore them. This will lead to a progressive loss of vision in that eye, which may become irreversible if not treated in time.

Frequently Asked Questions

The main causes of amblyopia

Strabismus: permanent deviation of one eye; Refractive disorders (visual defects) that are not yet corrected by wearing glasses: strong hyperopia, strong myopia, strong astigmatism in one or both eyes; Ocular morphological anomalies: congenital cataract, congenital glaucoma, genetic or hereditary pathologies; Congenital Ptosis (drooping of an eyelid in front of the pupillary axis).

What are the Risk factors for amblyopia?

Premature birth, Family history: amblyopia in one of the parents must be mentioned to the ophthalmologist, Certain neurological or developmental pathologies.

What is the Signs that your young child should seek help?

Child rubs his or her eyes Child squints or blinks frequently The child has a strabismus The child has eye pain or a headache The child has a learning delay or difficulty The child has recurrent chalazions The child has difficulty seeing at a distance or up close
